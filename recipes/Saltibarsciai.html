Saltibarsciai Recipe

Saltibarsciai is a traditional Lithuanian cold beet soup. It is a beloved dish in Lithuania and is often enjoyed during the summer months. The soup is made with a base of buttermilk and beetroot, giving it a vibrant pink color. Along with the beets, it is also flavored with cucumbers, dill, and hard-boiled eggs, adding a refreshing and savory taste to the soup. The combination of the cool temperature and the tangy flavors make Saltibarsciai a perfect dish to beat the summer heat. It is commonly served chilled and garnished with additional fresh dill and a dollop of sour cream. Whether you're looking for a light and refreshing appetizer or a satisfying main course, Saltibarsciai is a delightful choice that showcases the flavors of Lithuania's culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • Buttermilk
  • Beetroot
  • Cucumbers
  • Dill
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Sour cream (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Peel and grate the beetroot.
  2. Chop the cucumbers and dill.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the grated beetroot, chopped cucumbers, and dill.
  4. Add buttermilk to the bowl and mix well.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld.
  7. When ready to serve, garnish with sliced hard-boiled eggs and a dollop of sour cream.
  8. Enjoy your authentic Lithuanian Saltibarsciai!
